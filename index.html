Mother [August 2, 2017]

What better way to dedicate my first ever homage on my first ever legitimate website than to spend it on my ma'?

She probably won't ever read this in her lifetime, TBH lol. But should that stop me? Nah'. Check it, being a single-parent is hard, man. However, being a single-parent with two children at significantly different age timleines, while being financially unstable is insanely harder. Never her fault, she pushed on and adapted to her environment because to her being comfortable was knowing that her children were comfortable and she succeeded with what little resources she had. She raised her children correctly. She starved because her children were fed. She was cold because her children were warm. And she cried because her children were happy. Despite being physically and emotionally uncomfortable, she was mentally and spiritually comfortable because we were PEMS comfortable.

Ain't no mother better than a mother whose others are together even in dark weather.

I work hard so that one day I can just come close to giving back to her. One day I will take her to the Vatican, so that growing up in the streets of Manila just to be impoverished in the States in order for her children to have better opportunities will be worthwhile.

One day, ma'. Un día. Isang araw.

Two for:

Comfortability and Adaptability: the Genesis of Revelation [August 1, 2017]

So this summer I stayed in Plano, Texas for multiple reasons:

    I was living in Jester East at UT Austin, so I definitely had to leave the dormitory as you're not allowed to stay past the end of finals unless you want to pay in the thousands... a crime..

    I needed a job..

    I'm in a long-distance relationship..

    I'm not down to live in a pre-war studio with two other people in Manhattan... nor am I down to brave the thousand degree heat in the Rio Grande Valley, despite there being so much to do.. /s

Much like engineering, life is all about trade-offs. Sometimes we have to give up one to gain another. We all live unique lives, but the way it is lived - regardless of our spiritual state - is up to us embodying the ability to adapt to our environment for one clear purpose: being comfortable -- whether it is forged for physical, mental, emotional, and/or spiritual purposes. All of us - regardless of our earthly financial state - live on a currency called time, and this currency is borrowed.

It is up to us to decide how we want to be comfortable and then decide when to adapt to reach this state of comfortability, as we watch our share of borrowed time leave our coffers.

I Got It Made - Special Ed (1989) [July 31, 2017]

"I talk sense condensed into the form of a poem // Full of knowledge from my toes to the top of my dome // I'm kinda young -- but my tongue speaks maturity // I'm not a child, I don't need nothing for security"

Born and raised in Brooklyn, Edward Archer (Special Ed) was only 16 when this song released. I Got It Made -off of the Youngest in Charge album- became a mega-hit. Special Ed's gift of speech is definitely a blessing and really helped paved the way for cats in the golden era of hip hop.

His tone just embodies all aspects of being calm, cool, and collect. Lyricism aside... his tone is what, in my opinion, helps a writer portray their message in a way everybody can become engulfed.
